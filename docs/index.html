Extended Reality for Future Generations: Rethinking Design Patterns, Ethical Challenges, and New Use Cases

At this CHI ’26 proposed Workshop, attendees would think deeply about the potential harms and challenges of ubiquitous XR for future generations. Through short talks, group brainstorming, and speculative design sessions, participants from XR, interaction design, child–computer interaction, and education will surface emerging risks, anticipate dystopian scenarios, and envision responsible alternatives.

Draft Workshop Schedule
Activity Duration Description
Session 1
Welcome 5 min. Introduction of organizers and agenda
Introductions 10 min. Participants introduce themselves shortly to the other attendees
Discussing Harmful Developments 20 min. Groups brainstorm how future XR technology could create new challenges and harms, particularly to children and young adults. Harms, implications, and affected user groups will be gathered across groups.
Future XR: A Dystopy 60 min. Participants mix and regroup based on one or more future scenarios. Following a speculative design fiction activity they use the ideas from the previous session to develop scenarios and personas illustrating a dystopian future.
Present & Share 15 min. Each group shares their ideas in their preferred way (as visual, text, play, etc.)
Session 2
Welcome Back 5 min. Welcoming participants back from the break and repeating the main ideas from session 1
Discussing Positive Developments 20 min. Groups brainstorm how responsible research and development could avert the harmful situations discussed in session 1 while also strengthening the benefits of ubiquitous immersive experiences. A focus is again on future generations, children, and young adults. Ideas are collected across groups.
Future XR: A Utopy 60 min. Participants mix and regroup based on one or more future scenarios. Following a speculative design fiction activity they use the ideas from the previous session to develop scenarios and personas illustrating a utopian future.
Present & Share 15 min. Each group shares their ideas in their preferred way (as visual, text, play, etc.)
Closing Remarks 10 min. Summarizing the workshop, linking it to other sessions at CHI 2026, and providing time for networking

Proposal Abstract

Extended reality (XR) technology is rapidly advancing, with first headsets featuring viewer-only displays built into regular glasses. This trend shows how personalized augmented reality, visible only to individual users, could become reality in a few years, ultimately replacing smartphone-based computing. While such ubiquitous XR has many benefits and use cases, it also introduces novel challenges and potential harms, in particular for future XR-first generations and young users. In this workshop, we want to unite forces among researchers from various domains, including XR, interaction design, child-computer interaction, and education, to discuss and speculate about future scenarios. To attend, participants will submit a position paper reflecting on their ideas for a sustainable future enriched---not destroyed---by the spread of XR. In speculative design activities, attendees will collect upcoming harms, create dystopian scenarios, discuss approaches towards responsible, purposeful, and equitable XR innovations, and illustrate them finally through utopian scenarios. Through these activities, we aim to extend and foster our community of researchers committed to responsible XR design, established in prior workshops.
